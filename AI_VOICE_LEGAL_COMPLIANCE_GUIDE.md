## Executive Summary

### Critical Dates
- **February 8, 2024:** FCC declares AI-generated voices are "artificial" under TCPA (effective immediately)
- **January 27, 2025:** One-to-One Consent Rule takes effect
- **April 11, 2025:** New Opt-Out Rules take effect (10-day maximum response time)
- **January 1, 2026:** California AI Transparency Act takes effect

### Key Takeaway
**AI-generated voice calls are NOW regulated under the TCPA and require prior express consent.** Businesses using Voice AI agents must obtain proper consent, provide clear disclosures, honor opt-outs, and maintain detailed records.

## FCC AI Robocall Rules (2024-2025)

### February 2024 Declaratory Ruling

On **February 8, 2024**, the FCC unanimously ruled that:
- AI-generated voices are considered "artificial" under the TCPA
- This ruling applies to voice cloning technology and AI-generated speech
- The rule took effect **immediately**
- Prior express consent is required for all AI-generated voice calls

**Source:** [FCC Makes AI-Generated Voices in Robocalls Illegal](https://www.fcc.gov/document/fcc-makes-ai-generated-voices-robocalls-illegal)

### August 2024 Proposed Rules (NPRM)

On **August 7, 2024**, the FCC proposed additional requirements:

1. **Definition of AI-Generated Calls:**
   - "A call that uses any technology or tool to artificially generate a voice or text using computational technology or other machine learning, including predictive algorithms, and large language models, to process natural language and produce voice or text content to communicate with a called party over an outbound telephone call."

2. **Two New Requirements (Proposed):**
   - **Prior Express Written Consent:** Must obtain PEWC specifically for using AI-generated content (separate from general call consent)
   - **AI Disclosure Requirement:** Must "clearly disclose" the use of AI at the beginning of each call or text

3. **Timeline:**
   - Comment period closed October 25, 2024
   - Rules expected to be adopted in 2025
   - Implementation timeline TBD after adoption

**Source:** [FCC Proposes First AI-Generated Robocall & Robotext Rules](https://www.fcc.gov/document/fcc-proposes-first-ai-generated-robocall-robotext-rules-0)

### Accessibility Exemptions

People with hearing and speech disabilities are **exempt** from consent or identification requirements when:
- They use AI-generated voice to communicate during outbound calls
- The call does NOT contain any unsolicited advertisement

## TCPA Compliance Requirements

### 1. Prior Express Consent Requirements

#### Consent Types by Call Type:

| Call Type | Consent Level Required |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Informational calls | Prior express consent |
| Debt collection calls | Prior express consent |
| Marketing/telemarketing calls | Prior express **written** consent |
| AI-generated voice calls (ANY type) | Prior express consent (minimum) |
| AI marketing calls | Prior express **written** consent |

**Source:** [TCPA Consent Requirements](https://www.fdic.gov/resources/supervision-and-examinations/consumer-compliance-examination-manual/documents/8/viii-5-1.pdf)

#### Definition of Prior Express Written Consent (PEWC)

PEWC must include:
- Agreement in writing (electronic signatures valid under E-SIGN Act)
- Signature of the person called
- Clear authorization to deliver messages using ATDS or artificial/prerecorded voice
- The specific telephone number to receive calls
- Clear and conspicuous disclosure of what they're consenting to
- Statement that consent is NOT required as a condition of purchase

**Source:** [TCPA Express Consent Guide](https://www.dnc.com/blog/what-tcpa-express-consent)

### 2. One-to-One Consent Rule (Effective January 27, 2025)

**NEW:** The FCC's One-to-One Consent Rule requires:
- Consent must be obtained for **one seller at a time**
- Cannot obtain blanket consent for "partners" or multiple sellers
- Content must be "logically and topically associated" with the consent interaction

**NOTE:** This rule was **vacated by the 11th Circuit Court of Appeals**. Monitor for further regulatory action.

**Source:** [FCC One-to-One Consent Rule](https://docs.fcc.gov/public/attachments/DOC-408396A1.pdf)

### 3. Do Not Call Registry Compliance

**Requirements:**
- Do NOT call numbers on the National Do Not Call Registry
- Access the DNC Registry at least every **31 days**
- Maintain written procedures for DNC compliance
- Keep internal DNC list of opt-out requests
- Document all registry scrubbing activities

**Exemptions:**
- Tax-exempt nonprofits (but must still honor DNC Registry)
- Political campaigns and PACs
- Calls to established business relationships

**Source:** [TCPA DNC Compliance Guide](https://www.tcn.com/complete-guide-to-tcpa-compliance/)

### 4. Time-of-Day Restrictions ("Quiet Hours")

**Federal Rule:**
- NO calls before **8:00 AM** or after **9:00 PM** (called party's local time)
- Applies to all "telephone solicitations"

**State Variations:**
- **Florida:** No calls before 8 AM or after **8 PM** (stricter than federal)
- **Some states:** No calls on Sundays or public holidays

**Safe Harbor Time Window:**
- If time zone uncertain, call between **11:00 AM - 9:00 PM ET**
- This ensures compliance across all US time zones

**Source:** [TCPA Calling Hours Guide](https://activeprospect.com/blog/tcpa-calling-hours/)

### 5. Caller Identification Requirements

Must provide at the beginning of each call:
- Caller's name or identity
- Entity they represent
- Telephone number or address
- Purpose of the call

For AI-generated calls, must also disclose (proposed rule):
- That the call uses AI-generated voice

**Source:** [FCC TCPA Rules](https://www.fcc.gov/general/telemarketing-and-robocalls)

### 6. Opt-Out Requirements (New Rules April 11, 2025)

**Effective April 11, 2025:**
- Must honor opt-out requests within **10 business days** (maximum)
- Must accept multiple opt-out keywords:
  - "STOP", "QUIT", "END", "REVOKE", "OPT-OUT", "CANCEL", "UNSUBSCRIBE"
- Consumers can revoke consent "in any reasonable manner"
- Must provide opt-out mechanism within **2 seconds** of required disclosures

**Source:** [TCPA Opt-Out Rules 2025](https://www.bclplaw.com/en-US/events-insights-news/the-tcpas-new-opt-out-rules-take-effect-on-april-11-2025-what-does-this-mean-for-businesses.html)

## Call Recording Laws

### Two-Party Consent States (All-Party Consent)

The following **13-14 states** require ALL parties to consent to call recording:

1. California
2. Connecticut
3. Delaware
4. Florida
5. Illinois
6. Maryland
7. Massachusetts
8. Michigan
9. Montana
10. Nevada
11. New Hampshire
12. Pennsylvania
13. Vermont
14. Washington

**Source:** [Two-Party Consent States 2025](https://worldpopulationreview.com/state-rankings/two-party-consent-states)

### Key State-Specific Notes

**California:**
- Strictest laws in the nation
- California Invasion of Privacy Act (CIPA)
- Violations can be a **felony**
- Civil lawsuits allowed
- Applies to "confidential communications"

**Connecticut:**
- Criminal law: One-party consent
- Civil law: Allows lawsuits if all parties don't agree
- **Treat as all-party consent to be safe**

**Nevada:**
- Phone calls: All-party consent required
- In-person conversations: One-party consent

**Montana:**
- All parties must know they're being recorded
- Don't need explicit "yes" if everyone was clearly informed

**Source:** [State Call Recording Laws](https://www.avoma.com/blog/call-recording-laws)

### One-Party Consent States

**37 states + Washington D.C.** allow recording if at least one party consents (typically the recorder).

### Federal Baseline

**Federal Wiretap Act (18 U.S.C. ยง 2511):**
- Sets baseline of one-party consent
- States can impose stricter requirements
- Federal law is the floor, not the ceiling

### Interstate Call Recording

**CRITICAL RULE:** If participants are in different states:
- Apply the **strictest state's law**
- If ANY party is in a two-party consent state, get ALL parties' consent
- Document consent from all participants to be safe

**Source:** [Recording Phone Calls Guide](https://www.justia.com/50-state-surveys/recording-phone-calls-and-conversations/)

## State-Specific AI Regulations

### Colorado AI Act (SB 24-205)

**Effective Date:** February 1, 2026 (delayed from original June 2026 date)

**Key Requirements:**
- **Impact Assessments:** Required for "high-risk" AI systems
- **Consumer Disclosure:** Consumers must be aware they're interacting with an AI system
- **High-Risk AI Disclosure:** Must notify consumers before AI makes a "consequential decision"
- **Developer Disclosure:** Must publicly display high-risk AI systems on website

**What Qualifies as High-Risk:**
- AI that makes consequential decisions about consumers
- Decisions affecting education, employment, financial services, government services, healthcare, housing, legal services

**Source:** [Colorado AI Act Summary](https://www.skadden.com/insights/publications/2024/06/colorados-landmark-ai-act)

### California AI Transparency Act (SB 942)

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026

**Key Requirements:**
- Applies to "Covered Providers" (1M+ monthly visitors/users, publicly accessible in CA)
- Must provide free AI content detection tool
- Must include "latent" disclosures in AI-generated content
- Must offer option for "manifest" disclosure
- Must revoke third-party licenses within 96 hours if they can't include disclosures

**Penalties:**
- $5,000 per day for violations
- Enforced by CA Attorney General, city attorneys, county counsel

**Source:** [California AI Transparency Law](https://www.jonesday.com/en/insights/2024/10/california-enacts-ai-transparency-law-requiring-disclosures-for-ai-content)

### California AB 2013 (Training Data Disclosure)

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026

**Key Requirements:**
- Developers must publicly post training data information
- Applies to ALL generative AI systems made available to Californians
- No quantitative threshold (unlike SB 942)

**Source:** [California AB 2013](https://www.cooley.com/news/insight/2024/2024-10-16-californias-new-ai-laws-focus-on-training-data-content-transparency)

### California - Business Communications

**Existing Law:**
- If AI promotes goods/services to California public
- Must disclose non-human nature at start: **"I am an automated virtual assistant"**

**Source:** [California AI Bot Disclosure](https://softcery.com/lab/us-voice-ai-regulations-founders-guide)

### Other States with Proposed AI Disclosure Laws

**States with pending legislation** (as of 2024):
- Hawaii
- Idaho
- Illinois
- Massachusetts
- New York

These states are considering laws requiring chatbot/AI disclosure or holding providers accountable for misleading communications.

**Source:** [State AI Disclosure Laws](https://softcery.com/lab/us-voice-ai-regulations-founders-guide)

### Illinois - Biometric Information Privacy Act (BIPA)

**Special Requirement for Voice AI:**
- Requires **informed written consent** for any biometric identifier (including voiceprints)
- Must have **public retention policy**
- Separate consent document required for Illinois users
- Applies BEFORE capturing or analyzing voiceprints

**Source:** [Illinois BIPA for Voice AI](https://softcery.com/lab/us-voice-ai-regulations-founders-guide)

## Required Disclosures

### 1. AI Identity Disclosure (Current Requirement)

**At the beginning of EVERY AI-generated call, disclose:**
- Your company name
- Your telephone number
- **That the call uses AI-generated voice** (best practice, soon to be required)

**Example Script:**
> "Hello, this is an automated AI assistant calling from Capture Client. Our number is 865-346-3339. This call is being made using artificial intelligence technology. [Purpose of call...]"

### 2. Call Recording Disclosure

**For Two-Party Consent States:**

**Method 1 - Verbal Notification (Most Common):**
> "This call is being recorded for quality assurance and training purposes. By continuing this call, you consent to the recording."

**Method 2 - Automated Pre-Call Message:**
> "Thank you for calling [Company]. This call may be recorded for quality and training purposes. If you do not wish to be recorded, please hang up now."

**Method 3 - Beep Tone (California-Compliant):**
- Audible beep tone at regular intervals throughout conversation
- Must be clearly audible to all parties

**Source:** [Call Recording Disclosure Best Practices](https://www.openphone.com/blog/call-recording-disclosure/)

### 3. Opt-Out Mechanism Disclosure

**For marketing/telemarketing calls:**
> "If you would like to opt out of future calls, you can say 'stop', reply with 'STOP' to text messages, or call us at [number] to be added to our do-not-call list."

**Must provide within 2 seconds of identity disclosure.**

### 4. Purpose of Call Disclosure

**State the purpose clearly:**
- Informational call: "We're calling to confirm your appointment..."
- Sales call: "We're calling to tell you about our special offer..."
- Debt collection: "This is an attempt to collect a debt..."

### 5. Privacy Policy Reference (Best Practice)

**Include in pre-call recording or early in call:**
> "For information about how we handle your data, please visit our privacy policy at [website]."

## Penalties and Enforcement

### TCPA Penalties

**Per-Violation Fines:**
- **Non-willful violation:** $500 per call/text
- **Willful/knowing violation:** Up to $1,500 per call/text (treble damages)
- **No cap on total penalties**

**Example Settlement:**
- Capital One (2014): **$75.5 million** for ATDS violations

**Source:** [TCPA Penalties Guide](https://www.dnc.com/blog/what-are-penalties-associated-tcpa-violations)

### FCC Enhanced Penalties (TRACED Act)

**For intentional violations:**
- Up to **$10,000 per call** for intentional robocall violations
- Enhanced FCC authority under TRACED Act

**Source:** [TCPA Enforcement Overview](https://natlawreview.com/article/what-tcpa-heres-quick-background-americas-anti-robocall-statute-every-call-center)

### Class Action Risk

- TCPA has **4-year statute of limitations**
- High-volume callers can face **billions in damages** in class actions
- TCPA plaintiffs' bar actively files suits
- Average **250,000 TCPA complaints per month** to FTC

**Source:** [TCPA Class Action Risk](https://activeprospect.com/blog/tcpa-damages/)

### State Attorney General Enforcement

- **48 states** have MOUs with FCC to enforce TCPA violations
- State AGs can pursue violations under state law
- Additional state-level penalties may apply

**Source:** [FCC State Enforcement](https://www.wsgr.com/en/insights/fcc-rules-ai-generated-voices-are-artificial-under-the-tcpa.html)

### Private Right of Action

- Individuals can sue for TCPA violations
- No requirement to show actual harm
- Statutory damages awarded per violation
- Attorneys' fees may be awarded to prevailing plaintiffs

### California-Specific Penalties

**SB 942 (AI Transparency Act):**
- **$5,000 per day** for violations
- Enforced by CA Attorney General, city attorneys, county counsel

**CIPA (Call Recording):**
- Violations can be criminal **felony**
- Civil lawsuits for damages
- Statutory damages available

**Source:** [California AI Penalties](https://www.jonesday.com/en/insights/2024/10/california-enacts-ai-transparency-law-requiring-disclosures-for-ai-content)

### Illinois BIPA Penalties

- **$1,000 per negligent violation**
- **$5,000 per intentional/reckless violation**
- Private right of action (no actual harm required)
- No cap on total damages

### Do Not Call Registry Violations

- FTC penalties: Up to **$43,792 per call** for DNC violations

**Source:** [DNC Penalties](https://www.tratta.io/blog/tcpa-compliance-rules-guide)

## Summary of Key Compliance Requirements

### MUST DO (Legal Requirements)

1. **Obtain prior express consent** before making AI-generated calls
2. **Obtain prior express WRITTEN consent** for marketing/telemarketing calls
3. **Disclose AI use** at beginning of calls (proposed rule, best practice now)
4. **Honor Do Not Call Registry** (scrub every 31 days)
5. **Respect quiet hours** (8 AM - 9 PM local time)
6. **Provide opt-out mechanism** (process within 10 days by April 2025)
7. **Disclose call recording** in two-party consent states
8. **Identify caller** (company name, number, purpose)
9. **Maintain records** of consent, calls, opt-outs (4+ years)
10. **Comply with state AI laws** (California, Colorado, Illinois, etc.)

### BEST PRACTICES (Recommended)

1. **Over-communicate:** Disclose AI use even when not required
2. **Get written consent:** Even for non-marketing calls (easier to prove)
3. **Record consent:** Audio/screen recordings of consent process
4. **Implement "human on demand":** Allow instant transfer to human
5. **Use conservative calling hours:** 9 AM - 8 PM to be extra safe
6. **Scrub DNC weekly:** More frequent than 31-day requirement
7. **Process opt-outs faster:** Within 24-48 hours instead of 10 days
8. **Conduct regular audits:** Monthly compliance reviews
9. **Train AI thoroughly:** Include edge cases and compliance scenarios
10. **Maintain compliance insurance:** TCPA defense coverage

### HIGH-RISK ACTIONS TO AVOID

1. **Never call without consent**
2. **Never ignore opt-out requests**
3. **Never call DNC Registry numbers** (without exemption)
4. **Never call outside 8 AM - 9 PM local time**
5. **Never record without disclosure** (in two-party states)
6. **Never claim to be human** when you're AI
7. **Never collect voiceprints without consent** (especially Illinois)
8. **Never buy/sell consumer data** without proper consent chain
9. **Never use "blanket" consent** (must be one-to-one by Jan 2025)
10. **Never ignore state-specific requirements**
