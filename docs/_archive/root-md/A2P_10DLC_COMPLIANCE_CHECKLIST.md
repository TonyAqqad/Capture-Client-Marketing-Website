## A2P 10DLC REGISTRATION REQUIREMENTS

### What is 10DLC?
10DLC (10-Digit Long Code) is a new standard for business SMS messaging. All organizations must register their 10DLC numbers with The Campaign Registry (TCR). **Unregistered numbers are now blocked by US carriers, effective February 1, 2025.**

### ‚úÖ MANDATORY REGISTRATION CHECKLIST

- [ ] **Brand Registration** - Register your business entity with TCR
  - Legal business name (exactly as registered with IRS)
  - Employer Identification Number (EIN)
  - Business entity type (LLC, Corporation, etc.)
  - Business address
  - Business website URL
  - Tax ID verification

- [ ] **Campaign Registration** - Register each messaging campaign separately
  - Campaign name and detailed description
  - Campaign use case (Customer Service, Marketing, Alerts, etc.)
  - Sample messages (3-5 unique examples)
  - Call-to-Action description (minimum 40 characters)
  - Opt-in process documentation
  - Message volume estimates

- [ ] **Authentication+ (2025 Requirement)**
  - Tax ID verification
  - Company registration documents
  - Domain-based email verification
  - Additional business documentation

- [ ] **Reseller ID** (if applicable)
  - Required if registering campaigns on behalf of another entity
  - Cannot be changed after registration

- [ ] **Website & Privacy Policy**
  - Active business website required
  - A2P 10DLC-compliant privacy policy published on website
  - Privacy policy must be easily accessible from homepage

### ‚è±Ô∏è TIMELINE
- **Brand Registration**: 1-3 days (if information is accurate)
- **Campaign Registration**: 3-7 business days (if information is complete)
- **Total Process**: 1-3 weeks average

### üö´ RESTRICTED INDUSTRIES
The following industries are NOT eligible for 10DLC registration:
- Cannabis/Hemp
- Firearms
- Payday loans
- Third-party debt collection
- S.H.A.F.T. categories (Sex, Hate, Alcohol without age gating, Firearms, Tobacco, Vape/E-cigs)

### üí∞ FEES
- **Standard Brand Registration**: $4 one-time fee
- **Campaign Registration**: $10-$15 per campaign
- **Carrier pass-through fees**: Vary by volume

### ‚ö†Ô∏è CONSEQUENCES OF NON-COMPLIANCE
- SMS messages blocked by carriers
- Delivery failures
- High carrier fees ($10 per message)
- Suspension of messaging services
- Permanent bans from sending texts

### üìå ALTERNATIVE OPTION
**Toll-Free Numbers** can bypass A2P 10DLC requirements:
- Free registration process
- Less complex than 10DLC
- Still requires campaign registration
- No EIN/tax verification required

## STIR/SHAKEN COMPLIANCE

### What is STIR/SHAKEN?
STIR/SHAKEN is caller ID authentication that verifies a caller's identity to combat illegal call spoofing.

### ‚úÖ COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST

- [ ] **Voice Service Provider (VSP) Compliance**
  - Your VSP must be STIR/SHAKEN compliant
  - Verify VSP has filed Robocall Mitigation Plan with FCC
  - VSP must have SPC Token and digital certificate
  - Confirm VSP implementation in FCC Robocall Mitigation Database

- [ ] **Attestation Level Achievement**
  - Target: **Level A** (Full Attestation) - lowest spam risk
  - Level B: Partial Attestation
  - Level C: Gateway Attestation (highest spam risk)

- [ ] **Third-Party Authentication** (if applicable)
  - Written agreement with third-party provider
  - Agreement must specify tasks performed
  - Provider makes all attestation-level decisions
  - Calls signed using your certificate (not third party's)

- [ ] **Record Keeping**
  - Maintain records of third-party authentication agreements
  - Keep documentation of attestation levels assigned
  - Track call authentication success rates

### üìÖ KEY COMPLIANCE DATES
- **February 26, 2024**: All VSPs must file Robocall Mitigation Plans
- **November 2024**: Third-party authentication rules in effect
- **Ongoing**: Continuous compliance required

### ‚úÖ BUSINESS ACTIONS REQUIRED
- [ ] Verify your VoIP/calling provider is STIR/SHAKEN compliant
- [ ] Request attestation level documentation
- [ ] Consider signing your own calls for end-to-end authentication
- [ ] Monitor call answer rates (Level A improves answer rates)

## TCPA COMPLIANCE FOR AI VOICE AGENTS

### FCC Ruling: AI Voices = Artificial Voices (February 2024)
The FCC confirmed that **AI-generated voices are "artificial voices" under the TCPA** and require prior express consent.

### ‚úÖ CORE COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS

- [ ] **Prior Express Consent for AI Voice Calls**
  - Obtain written consent before making AI voice calls
  - Consent must be specific to automated/AI-generated calls
  - Cannot make AI calls without explicit permission
  - Applies to ALL AI technologies (voice cloning, interactive AI, generic AI voices)

- [ ] **Prior Express WRITTEN Consent for Telemarketing**
  - Marketing calls using AI require written consent
  - Consent must include signature or electronic agreement
  - Must clearly state consent is for marketing purposes

- [ ] **Live Agent Presence Doesn't Exempt Compliance**
  - Even if a live agent controls the AI voice, TCPA still applies
  - Interactive AI responding to questions = TCPA covered
  - AI impersonation/voice cloning = TCPA covered

### ‚úÖ CALL IDENTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS

- [ ] **Caller ID Display**
  - Display accurate caller ID information
  - Include business name or recognizable identifier
  - Never spoof or falsify caller ID

- [ ] **Verbal Identification** (at start of call)
  - Clearly state your business name
  - Explain the purpose of the call
  - Provide callback number

- [ ] **AI Disclosure** (PROPOSED - implement now as best practice)
  - Disclose at the start that AI technology is being used
  - Example: "This is an AI-powered call from [Business Name]..."
  - Expected to become mandatory in 2025

### ‚úÖ OPT-OUT MECHANISMS

- [ ] **Easy Opt-Out Methods** (New Rules Effective April 11, 2025)
  - Allow recipients to say "stop," "quit," "end," "revoke," "opt out," "cancel," or "unsubscribe"
  - Honor opt-out requests within 10 business days
  - Provide opt-out instructions during call
  - Send one-time confirmation of opt-out (SMS only)

- [ ] **Do Not Call Registry Compliance**
  - Scrub calling lists against National DNC Registry
  - Maintain internal DNC list
  - Never call numbers on DNC lists

- [ ] **Call Time Restrictions**
  - Only call between 8:00 AM - 9:00 PM (recipient's local time zone)
  - Never call outside legal hours
  - Respect time zone differences

### üìú REQUIRED CONSENT LANGUAGE FOR AI CALLS

**Recommended Consent Text**:
```
"I agree to receive calls, which may include AI-generated voice messages
and automated calls, from [Company Name] at the phone number provided.
I understand that:
- Calls may be made using an automatic telephone dialing system
- AI technology may be used to generate voice messages
- I am not required to provide this consent as a condition of purchase
- I can revoke this consent at any time by saying 'stop' or contacting
  [Company Name] at [phone/email]
- Message and data rates may apply"
```

### ‚ö†Ô∏è PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE
- **$500 - $1,500 per violation** (per call/message)
- No maximum limit on statutory damages
- Class action lawsuits from consumers
- FCC enforcement actions
- State Attorney General prosecutions

### üìå ENFORCEMENT EXAMPLES
- **August 2024**: Lingo Telecom fined **$1 million** for AI-generated robocalls impersonating President Biden without disclosure
- **48+ State AGs**: Memoranda of understanding with FCC to enforce TCPA violations

## RECORD KEEPING REQUIREMENTS

### ‚úÖ DOCUMENTATION YOU MUST MAINTAIN

#### 1. CONSENT RECORDS

- [ ] **What to Keep**:
  - Exact date and time consent was obtained
  - Method of consent (web form, SMS keyword, phone call, etc.)
  - IP address (for online opt-ins)
  - Exact consent language shown to user
  - User's signature or electronic agreement
  - Form screenshots or page captures

- [ ] **How Long to Keep**: Minimum 4 years (recommended: 7 years)

- [ ] **Storage Method**:
  - Secure, encrypted database
  - Tamper-proof audit trail
  - Easily retrievable for legal defense

#### 2. OPT-OUT RECORDS

- [ ] **What to Keep**:
  - Date and time of opt-out request
  - Method of opt-out (text, call, email, etc.)
  - Exact opt-out message/request
  - Date opt-out was honored
  - System where opt-out was applied

- [ ] **How Long to Keep**: Indefinitely (to prevent re-contact)

#### 3. CALL/MESSAGE LOGS

- [ ] **What to Keep**:
  - Every call attempt (success or failure)
  - Every message sent
  - Timestamp (with time zone)
  - Recipient phone number
  - Message content or call script
  - Call duration and outcome
  - AI vs. human agent indicator

- [ ] **How Long to Keep**: 4-7 years

#### 4. TCR/10DLC REGISTRATION DOCUMENTS

- [ ] **What to Keep**:
  - Brand registration confirmation
  - Campaign registration approval
  - Sample messages submitted
  - Call-to-Action descriptions
  - Any vetting or verification documents

- [ ] **How Long to Keep**: Duration of campaign + 7 years

#### 5. THIRD-PARTY AGREEMENTS

- [ ] **What to Keep** (if using third-party providers):
  - Service agreements with SMS/voice providers
  - Data processing agreements
  - STIR/SHAKEN third-party authentication agreements

- **Criminal penalties** for egregious violations
- **License revocation** (for telecom providers)
