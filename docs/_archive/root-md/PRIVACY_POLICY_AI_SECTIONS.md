## Call Recording Privacy

This section explains how we handle call recordings and your rights
regarding recorded calls.

### When Calls Are Recorded

**Calls may be recorded when:**
- You consent to recording at the beginning of the call
- Recording is legally permitted in your jurisdiction
- Recording is necessary for legitimate business purposes
- You are notified and given the option to decline

**Calls are NOT recorded when:**
- You decline recording consent
- You are in a jurisdiction where recording is prohibited
- You have opted out of call recording
- Technical issues prevent recording

### Legal Basis for Recording

**We record calls based on:**
- Your consent (primary basis)
- Legitimate business interests (quality assurance, compliance)
- Legal obligations (regulatory compliance, legal defense)
- Contract performance (fulfilling our service obligations)

### State Law Compliance

**One-Party Consent States (37 states + DC):**
In these states, we may record calls with the consent of one party (us).
However, we still notify you as a courtesy and best practice.

**Two-Party Consent States (All-Party Consent):**
In California, Connecticut, Delaware, Florida, Illinois, Maryland,
Massachusetts, Michigan, Montana, Nevada, New Hampshire, Pennsylvania,
Vermont, and Washington, ALL parties must consent to recording.

**How we comply:**
- You will be explicitly notified at the beginning of recorded calls
- You will be asked for consent before recording begins
- If you decline, the call will not be recorded (or you may be transferred
  to a non-recorded line)
- Continued participation after notification constitutes consent

### Interstate Call Recording

When calls involve parties in different states:
- We apply the **most restrictive state's law**
- If ANY party is in a two-party consent state, we obtain all-party consent
- We notify all parties of recording regardless of state law

### How We Notify You of Recording

**Notification Methods:**

**Pre-Call Automated Message (Inbound Calls):**
> "Thank you for calling Capture Client. This call may be recorded for
> quality and training purposes. If you do not wish to be recorded, please
> press 1 now or hang up. Otherwise, please hold for the next available
> representative."

**Verbal Notification (Outbound Calls):**
> "Hello, this is [Name] from Capture Client on a recorded line. This call
> will be recorded for quality assurance and training purposes. By
> continuing this call, you consent to the recording. Is that okay?"

**Beep Tone (Alternative for California):**
- Periodic audible beep tone during the call
- Clearly audible to all parties
- Legally compliant alternative to verbal notification in California

## How Our AI Makes Decisions

We believe in transparency about how our AI voice technology works and
makes decisions.

### AI Decision-Making Process

**Our Voice AI follows this process:**

**1. Speech Recognition:**
- Converts your speech into text using automatic speech recognition (ASR)
- Processes audio in real-time
- Handles multiple accents and speech patterns
- Filters background noise

**2. Natural Language Understanding (NLU):**
- Analyzes the meaning and intent of your words
- Identifies key information (names, dates, requests)
- Understands context from conversation history
- Detects sentiment and emotion

**3. Decision Logic:**
- Applies business rules and workflows
- Consults knowledge bases and FAQs
- Considers your customer history and preferences
- Determines appropriate response or action

**4. Response Generation:**
- Creates human-like response using natural language generation (NLG)
- Personalizes based on conversation context
- Ensures accuracy and helpfulness
- Formats for clarity

**5. Speech Synthesis:**
- Converts text response to AI-generated voice
- Uses natural-sounding speech patterns
- Adjusts tone and pacing
- Delivers response to caller

### What Influences AI Responses

**AI responses are influenced by:**

**Your Input:**
- What you say during the call
- Your tone and sentiment
- Questions you ask
- Information you provide

**Conversation Context:**
- Previous messages in current call
- History of past calls with us
- Your customer profile and preferences
- Current task or goal

**Business Rules:**
- Pre-programmed workflows and policies
- Service availability and hours
- Appointment scheduling logic
- Escalation criteria
